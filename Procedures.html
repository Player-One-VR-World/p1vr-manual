Host Duties​

General Customer Procedure:

  1. Ask if the guest has a reservation or if they are a walk-in.
  2. Discuss E.W.A.R.
    • Experience
    • Worldwide
    • Arena
    • Ratings
info

E.W.A.R.​

E (Experience)
  • Inquire about their experience with VR.
  • Ask them to rate their experiences on a scale of 1-10 (with 5 being average). Our home VR is about a 2; our game rates between 7 and 10.
W (Worldwide)
  • Highlight our global VR game collection.
  • We offer the world's best VR games from Australia, Hong Kong, Hungary, Germany, Belgium, and Canada.
A (Arena)
  • Emphasize that we are the largest free-roam VR arena in California.
  • Our facility features multiple free roam rooms and over 5000 sq. ft., using the latest 5K technology and super-fast Wi-Fi 6e.
R (Ratings)
  • Explain our game ratings.
  • We rate games from 1-10, with 5 being average. Games rated 6 and below are not included in our library. Most VR places offer 6-8 games, with only a few rated 7 or higher. Any game on our website promises a great experience.
  1. If they have a reservation or are a walk-in, MUST confirm the game they are playing and discuss alternatives if they change their mind.
  2. Have them sign waivers and show them the DOB scrolling. Ensure they use different emails.
  3. Notify the GM via text or walkie-talkie about the game and the number of players.
  4. Have them wait in the designated area and confirm readiness with the GM.
  5. Bring them in and introduce them to the GM.

Game Master Duties / Information for Customers​

  1. Verify with the host whether the customers have paid or confirm payment in the system.
  2. Do not assume which game they want to play; ask them before starting.
  3. Ensure the headset and hand controllers are fully charged and functioning.
  4. Move the scooter if it’s in room 2.
  5. Instruct customers to place their belongings on the bench or in lockers with locks.
  6. Secure hand controllers (attach wrist straps).
  7. Brief them on how to play the game (reloading, objectives, item handling, etc.) and show the briefing video.
tip

Check that one or two headsets are at maximum volume, even if you pressed the buttons. Also, double-check that the lenses are clean!

  1. Put on the headset.
warning

Check batteries - Quest 3 headsets need a manual battery check as Another World doesn’t accurately report battery levels.

  1. Start the game.

Remember the acronym H.P.I.Q.

  • Headset
  • Payment
  • Intro
  • Questions
info

H.P.I.Q.​

H (Headset)
  • Check the volume and ensure lenses are wiped clean.
P (Payment)
  • Confirm that the customer has paid and that the number of players matches the transaction.
I (Intro)
  • Provide an introduction to the game and explain the controls.
Q (Questions)
  • Ask if the party has any questions.

Game Master Duties When the Game Is Over​

  1. Ask about their experience.
  2. Offer to take a picture together.
  3. Discuss T.E.R.M.
    • Tap
    • Easy5
    • Replay
    • Membership
info

T.E.R.M.​

T (Tap)
  • Offer a Google Review Tap and ask if they had a 5-star experience. If they did, provide a $5 coupon for a future visit in exchange for a 5-star review.
E (Easy5)
  • Give a $5 off coupon for their next visit.
R (Replay)
  • Offer a 20% discount for a second experience booked on the same day or for a future date. Also, ask if they want to try the 360 Ride for $20.
M (Membership)
  • Inform them about our membership program if they seem interested in returning.

Cleaning Procedure​

  1. Wipe down the headsets with an alcohol wipe.

  2. Place the headsets in the UV cabinet for the full duration.

  3. Spray a cloth with lavender deodorizer and wipe the headsets.

  4. Clean the lenses.

Company Rules​

  1. Lunch break is one hour. Keep track of your time in and out.
  2. Stagger lunch breaks if possible.
  3. Clock in and out on time.
  4. Do not clock in or out for someone else.
  5. Notify the team when taking a 10-minute break.
info

You are allowed one 10-minute break per 4-hour shift. If working 8 hours, try to take one break near the beginning and one later in your shift.

  1. Clean out the fridge before parties.
  2. Clean up after yourself following lunch.
  3. Keep drinks in enclosed containers.
  4. Ensure only one person communicates rules and experience information to each customer.
warning

Do not wear earbuds while working.

Party/Events​

  1. Prepare graphic name tags, hologram wall names, and birthday room signs.
  2. Email the waiver list to the host.
  3. Decorate the birthday room.
  4. Clean the bathroom and party room, ensuring tablecloths are clean.
  5. Prepare tip receipts.
  6. Provide name tags for each guest.
  7. Prepare ride tickets.
  8. Test all games on the day of the event to ensure headsets are working properly.
info

Legendary Parties​

These are events the entire place has been reserved, and they paid for our best package.

  • Prepare snacks and drinks, snacks in woven basket, put drinks in the fridge
  • Four clean tablecloths for legendary parties
  • Prepare middle tables in hallway at least 1 hour before
  • The group must be informed 24 hours prior about waiver links, or if they already did all of them say congratulations on completing all of the waivers.
