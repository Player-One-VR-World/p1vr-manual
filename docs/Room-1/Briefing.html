Briefing

Once the player's session has been created (see Step by Step Guide for how to do so), you can hand each player their respectively assigned gun controller.

After that you can explain how you are going to show them a short briefing video.

Example: "Alright now I'm going to play a quick briefing video; it will sort of explain the setting of the game you will be playing in as well as the necessary controls you will need to know. Afterwards, I'll review everything one last time and get any questions you may have."

The controller for the screen that plays the briefing video has the following buttons:

You can select the respective game using the left/right buttons and by hitting the select button.

After the video has ended, re-review the controls of the game, as well as parts of the briefing video they might have missed.

note

Relevant information you should give the player varies on a game-to-game basis; be sure to listen to others to pick up on what people might have failed to realize.

Some useful tidbits to remind them about:

  • When you die you turn gray for a little less than 10 seconds, and you respawn at the end. Feel free to move around while dead to get a better position.
  • Markers on the floor that are highlighted by a player's respective team color must be stepped on in order to progress the game. These can also be seen as boxes with footprints in them.
  • For Outbreak specifically, the special guns can be picked up by walking over the yellow circle underneath the floating gun.
    • Also for special weapons, one must empty their entire clip if they wish to pick up another special weapon on the floor, or it won't let them.

After this has all been explained to them, ask them if they have any questions.

Once you have answered them, you are ready to explain how to use the headsets. Run by them how to tighten/loosen the headset. Additionally show them about the eye distance knob on the front, and explain how they can try to use it if their vision is blurry.

Inform them they are going to be wearing headsets that allow them to communicate with eachother and with the Game Master if ever necessary.

Be sure to tell them if they have any problems whatsoever during the game, that they can call for a game master for help and they will provide assistance immediately.

Then you can either help them put on their headsets or let them take the one corresponding to the number of the gun they are wearing.

tip

For groups of children, put the headsets on for them. For older groups, you can let them put them on and help anyone who seems to be having issues. Use your own discretion.

Once they are in the lobby make sure all the fans are running at max speed (6).

Also unplug the Room 1 headset, turn it on, and make sure you can hear the game and the people playing. You will be using this to communicate with them if necessary.

warning

Make sure the headset microphone is in the upright position if you are not speaking, as the players will be able to hear you if it isn't.

info

On the scores monitor, make sure the Zero Latency viewer is open that shows that game being played. Do this regardless of if there are spectators or not, as there could be walk-ins that see this.

For information on how to use the scores monitor and PC, see - Scores Monitor

The Zero Latency viewing application should be open by default and you can Alt + Tab to said application. If it isn't open, then you can do so by clicking on the shortcut on the bottom right of the desktop.
