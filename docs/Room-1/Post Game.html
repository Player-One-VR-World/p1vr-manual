Post Game

Once the players have finished the game, you can speak into the microphone by pulling it down and explaining how they can now take off the headset.

Example: "Alright guys, you can take off your headsets now."

You should ask for their opinions of the game and have casual conversation about their impressions while taking their headsets.

It is also important to ask if they would like to take a group photo with the equipment, as this will give them a lasting memory of the time they spent here. You should ask to borrow their phone for the photo, so that we don't have to spend time later finding their phone number to share the photo.

Explain to them that you can pull up their scores in a minute.

The post game procedures can be found here: T.E.R.M.

warning

Tell them about T.E.R.M. BEFORE you show them their scores, as they will be less likely to give a review if they aren't waiting on anything and have the free time to do so.

Scores Monitor​

The score monitor will be where you show the players their scores.

If the monitor is not on, you can turn it on using the Samsung remote.

If the monitor doesn't automatically connect to the scores PC, the computer is probably off. You can turn on the scores PC by pressing the power button on the right most shelf of computers located above the Zero Latency headsets/guns.

The keyboard and mouse labelled "ZL SCORES" control this PC.

Navigating to Scores​

info

Keyboard/mouse input is very unresponsive and glitchy when the Zero Latency application is open. Feel free to close it if you can't manage to perform the tasks you want, and then just open it when you need too.

To reach the game scores, navigate to the Zero Latency portal. This website should automatically open when you open chrome, but if it doesn't, it should also be on the bookmarks bar. Click the sidebar Game Results button.

Click the View button located on the session just finished on the right most side.

Click the View On Website button located at the top of page.

You will be directed to the Zero Latency website, where you can see organized infographics of the post-game scores.
