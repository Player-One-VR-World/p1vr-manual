Briefing

Once the each headset has been booted into either Hero Launcher or VRCave (see Step by Step Guide for how to do so), you can hand each player their controllers.

Ensure each player has their controllers strapped to their wrists, and the controllers are properly connected to the headset.

After that you can give a brief explanation of the controls for their specific experience (see Equipment for an explanation of controls).

Example: "Here's a brief explanation on the controls and a small summary of the Escape Room experience you guys will be having today..."

In-game hints are available for players who decide to use them. They are activated by placing their controller on any lit-up blue hand signs displayed around the different rooms.

After this has all been explained to them, ask them if they have any questions.

Once you have answered them, you are ready to explain how to use the headsets. Run by them how to tighten/loosen the headset. Additionally show them about the eye distance knob on the front, and explain how they can try to use it if their vision is blurry.

Be sure to tell them if they have any problems whatsoever during the game, that they can call for a game master for help and they will provide assistance immediately.

Proceed to help each player put on their headset, ensuring proper comfortability.

tip

For groups of children, always put the headsets on for them. For older groups, you can let them put them on and help anyone who seems to be having issues. Use your own discretion. Always ensure proper fit as headsets falling off are the majority of accidents within this room.

Also unplug the Room 3 headset, turn it on, and make sure you can hear the game they are playing.

warning

This room does not use any external headphones, therefore you cannot communicate to them through the headset. Please speak to them physically within their room.
