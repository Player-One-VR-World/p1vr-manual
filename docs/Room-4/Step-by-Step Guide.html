Step-by-Step Guide

Turn on the number of headsets required (this can be done prior to booking time). Hold the power button for ~5 seconds until the white LED flashes on the front of the headset.

warning

Ensure the headset lenses are wiped and the eye distance knob is reset prior to handing a customer a headset!

Once each headset is turned on, double check both controllers are properly connected.

info

Each controller will signify it's connection by the LED light located at the top of the controller.
A green light is a properly connected controller.
A blue light means the controller cannot find the headset, but is turned on.
A red light signifies a dying controller (needs to be charged).

Adjust the volume button (the one closer to the nose), by hitting it ~12 times to ensure the headset is at max volume. You can put on the headset to ensure the volume is maxxed out.

warning

It is important to spot check one or two headsets before a group plays, as having max volume drastically increases the quality of the experience.

Obtain their Player Information from the google sheets adult/minor waiver forms. These are google sheets that are accessible from the bookmarks bar:

info

If the player does not have any specificed Gamer Display Name, enter their first name instead.
